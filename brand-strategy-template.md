# Brand Strategy Template — Worked Example + Reusable Schema

This is the "marketing brain" that sits in front of your content engine. It teaches the three things an agency strategist defines before anyone writes a word — **ICP, Pillars, Topic-Cluster Map** — using one fully worked brand, then a contrasting second brand, then the reusable schema and a step-by-step process to build one for *any* brand.

> Read the worked example first to learn the *shape*. Then use the schema (§4) as your database/config structure, and the process (§5) to fill it in from real evidence.

---

## 1. Worked example: "Fernwell" — a DTC houseplant & plant-care brand

*Online-first brand selling indoor plants, peat-free potting mix, tools, and a monthly "Plant Care Box" subscription. Ships nationally. We'll use this throughout because plant care has obvious, learnable structure.*

### 1A. ICP — Ideal Customer Profile

Lead with one primary ICP. (Fernwell has a smaller secondary one too — shown after.)

**Primary ICP — "The Anxious Beginner Plant Parent"**

| Field | Fernwell's answer |
|---|---|
| **Who they are** | 25–40, urban/suburban, renters or first-home owners, mid income, design-conscious. Heavy on Instagram, Pinterest, TikTok. |
| **Identity & values** | Wants their space to feel alive, calm, and "put-together." Cares about wellness; mildly about sustainability. Sees plants as self-care + aesthetic identity. |
| **Jobs to be done** | "Make my home feel warm and alive." "Keep a plant alive without feeling stupid." "Look like someone who has their life together." |
| **Pains / frustrations** | Has killed plants before. Overwhelmed by conflicting advice. Doesn't know watering/light basics. Afraid of wasting money. Put off by jargon. |
| **Buying triggers** | Moved into a new place; redecorating; a plant just died; gift occasion; saw a plant on social. |
| **Objections** | "I have a black thumb." "Too expensive." "Too much maintenance." "Will it survive shipping?" |
| **Awareness stage** | Mostly **problem-** and **solution-aware**, low **product-awareness**. Searches how-tos and "best plant for X" long before any brand name. |
| **Vocabulary (their words)** | "low-maintenance," "hard to kill," "low light," "pet-safe," "indirect light" — **not** "drought-tolerant xerophyte" or "well-draining substrate." |

> **Why the vocabulary row matters most:** it's simultaneously your *keyword list*, your *writing tone guide*, and your *anti-jargon filter*. Pull these phrases verbatim from real reviews and forums.

**Secondary ICP — "The Aspiring Collector"** (briefly): more experienced, hunts rare/variegated plants, higher lifetime value, product-aware, uses precise jargon. Served by a *small* slice of content (rare-plant care, propagation), not the main thrust. *Lesson: name secondary ICPs, but don't let them dilute the primary focus.*

### 1B. Pillars — the territory Fernwell wants to own

Three-to-five themes. Note how most serve top-of-funnel demand and exactly one serves the decision stage.

| # | Pillar | What it's about | Business goal | Funnel stage |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Plant Care & Troubleshooting** | How-to + problem-solving (yellow leaves, watering, light) | SEO authority + trust; captures huge informational search demand | Awareness → consideration *(the workhorse)* |
| 2 | **Choosing the Right Plant** | "Best plant for low light / pets / beginners / bathrooms" | Commercial-intent traffic that maps directly to products | Consideration *(high conversion)* |
| 3 | **Plant Styling & Home** | Styling, pots, rooms, aesthetics | Aspirational/identity content; social-shareable; brand affinity | Awareness + brand |
| 4 | **Sustainability & Sourcing** | Peat-free mix, ethical sourcing, plastic-pot problem | Trust + differentiation + values alignment | Consideration + brand |
| 5 | **The Fernwell Way** | The subscription box, the guarantee, why-us vs. big-box stores | Decision-stage conversion | Decision *(low volume, high intent)* |

> **The beginner trap:** making everything Pillar 5 (product/why-us). That content only reaches people who already know you — a tiny audience. Pillars 1–2 are where *strangers* find you. Aim ~70% of output at awareness/consideration, ~30% at decision/brand.

### 1C. Topic-Cluster Map — Pillar 1 fully expanded (hub-and-spoke)

This is the structure agencies call a **topic cluster**: one broad **hub** page that links to many specific **spoke** posts, and every spoke links back to the hub. The hub builds authority for the head term; spokes capture long-tail searches; internal links pass authority and route readers toward products.

**HUB:** *"The Complete Beginner's Guide to Keeping Houseplants Alive"*
— target keyword: `how to take care of houseplants` · intent: informational · funnel: awareness · links out to every spoke below.

**SPOKES:**

| Spoke post | Target keyword | Intent | Funnel | Links to | Maps to product | Distribution recipe |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Why are my Monstera leaves turning yellow? | `monstera leaves turning yellow` | Informational (problem) | Awareness | Hub + Monstera care kit | Care kit | Newsletter tip + IG carousel |
| How often should you water a snake plant? | `how often to water snake plant` | Informational | Awareness | Hub + Snake Plant page | Snake plant | Newsletter Q&A + short video |
| Best low-light indoor plants | `best low light indoor plants` | Commercial investigation | Consideration | Low-light collection + Hub | Collection | Newsletter feature + Pinterest |
| How to repot a houseplant (step by step) | `how to repot a plant` | Informational (how-to) | Awareness | Hub + Potting mix + Tools | Mix + tools | Newsletter how-to + Reel |
| Pet-safe houseplants that are hard to kill | `pet safe indoor plants` | Commercial | Consideration | Pet-safe collection + Hub | Collection | Newsletter + IG (maps to a known objection) |
| Overwatering vs underwatering: how to tell | `overwatering vs underwatering` | Informational | Awareness | Hub | — | Newsletter myth-buster |

*Repeat the same hub-and-spoke build for Pillars 2–5. A complete Fernwell strategy might be 5 hubs + ~50 spokes — that's a year-plus of on-strategy content the engine can work through.*

**Read the map left to right and you can see the whole machine:** each row already tells the pipeline the keyword to target, the angle (intent), the CTA to use (funnel stage), which internal links to add, which product to point at, and how to repurpose it. That's why Step 0 of the pipeline can "pick a topic" and everything downstream just inherits the context.

## 2. Contrasting example: "Ledgerly" — bookkeeping SaaS for freelancers

*Same skeleton, totally different brand — to prove it generalizes from B2C ecommerce to B2B lead-gen.*

**ICP — "The Tax-Dreading Solo Freelancer":** solo contractor/creative, ~$50–150k revenue, time-poor, hates admin, fears tax season. JTBD: "stop dreading taxes," "stop losing receipts." Pains: shoebox of receipts, no idea what's deductible. Triggers: tax season, a scary IRS letter, income jump, hiring an accountant who's too expensive. Objections: "I can just use a spreadsheet." Vocabulary: "write-offs," "quarterly taxes," "1099," "deductions."

**Pillars:** (1) *Freelance Taxes & Deductions* [awareness workhorse] · (2) *Bookkeeping Basics for Solos* [consideration] · (3) *Getting Paid / Invoicing* [awareness + affinity] · (4) *Spreadsheets vs Software / Ledgerly vs alternatives* [decision].

**Cluster (Pillar 1) — HUB:** *"The Freelancer's Guide to Taxes"* (`freelancer taxes`). **Spokes:** `freelance tax deductions list` (commercial), `how to pay quarterly taxes` (informational), `do I need an LLC as a freelancer` (informational), `self-employment tax calculator` (transactional/tool), `home office deduction freelancer` (informational).

Identical structure: ICP → pillars (mostly top-of-funnel, one decision) → hub-and-spoke clusters tagged with keyword/intent/funnel/product/distribution. **That sameness is the point** — your engine consumes the same schema regardless of brand.

## 3. The funnel → CTA mapping (the connective tissue)

Define this once per brand so the engine picks the right call-to-action automatically by a topic's funnel stage:

| Funnel stage | Reader mindset | Content type | Default CTA |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Awareness** | "I have a problem/question" | How-to, troubleshooting, definitions | Soft: subscribe to newsletter / free guide |
| **Consideration** | "What are my options?" | "Best X for Y," comparisons, buying guides | Medium: view a product collection / category |
| **Decision** | "Is *this* the right choice?" | Why-us, reviews, vs-competitor, guarantees | Hard: buy / start trial / subscribe to product |

Each `spoke` is one row in the `topics` table from the build plan — the handoff point between strategy and the production pipeline. The pipeline reads a spoke, produces content, and writes `status` + `published_url` back.

## 5. How to build this for any brand (the learnable process)

Do it from **evidence, not imagination** — this is the actual marketing skill.

1. **Find the ICP from real signals.** Read customer reviews (yours *and* competitors'), support/sales conversations, and forums/Reddit/comment sections where the audience complains. Copy exact phrases. Those become your `pains`, `objections`, and `vocabulary`. (If you have no customers yet, mine competitor reviews and niche communities.)

2. **Derive 3–5 pillars** at the intersection of *(ICP pains & jobs-to-be-done)* × *(what the business sells / wants to be known for)*. Keep it to 3–5. Most should target awareness/consideration; exactly one targets decision.

3. **Build clusters with keyword research inside each pillar.** For each pillar, find one broad **hub** keyword and 6–15 long-tail **spoke** keywords (DataForSEO, Google autocomplete, "People also ask," competitor headers). Group by *topic*, not by search volume.

4. **Tag every spoke** with intent, funnel stage, internal-link targets, the product it maps to, and a distribution recipe. This tagging is what lets the engine auto-pick CTAs, internal links, and repurposing.

5. **Decide a default distribution recipe** up front (e.g. *every pillar post → 1 newsletter section + 3 social snippets + internal links from 2 older posts*). Encode it so Step 7 of the pipeline runs automatically.

**Shortcut:** give Claude a tight brand brief (what you sell, who buys, top 3 competitors) and ask it to *draft* the ICP, pillars, and a starter cluster map in this schema — then edit against real reviews. Drafting-then-editing is far faster than a blank page and is the best way to learn the shape.

**Keep it alive.** This is a living document. Revisit quarterly: kill clusters that don't perform, expand the ones that do, and let performance data (GSC + MailerLite) drive what the engine writes next. That feedback loop — strategy → produce → measure → refine — *is* what an agency sells.
